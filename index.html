About

I am a PhD candidate in theoretical physics at Purdue University, working at the intersection of condensed matter theory and quantum information. My research focuses on quantum phase transitions in low-dimensional systems, combining field-theoretic techniques, Monte Carlo simulations, and machine-learning–based inference. I have 4 peer-reviewed publications in Physical Review B, Physical Review C, Physical Review E, and Journal of Physics, with 2 additional papers in progress. I have presented my work at multiple international conferences and have 7+ years of research experience in quantum phase transitions.

Research

1D–2D Phase Transitions & Superconductivity In Progress
Using Bosonization to demonstrate that geometric disorder enhances superconductivity in coupled quantum wire arrays, and developing a novel SNSPD device design. Also formulating a theory for the supersolid phase transition in ultracold atom arrays observed in tensor-network simulations. Applied field-theoretic techniques to model exotic correlated and topological phase transitions in optical lattice experiments.
Machine Learning for Quantum Materials In Progress
Building disorder-resilient CNN architectures trained on Monte Carlo data to infer Hamiltonian parameters and locate phase boundaries in quantum materials.
Quantum Phase Transitions in Transport
Theoretical study of a field-induced metal–insulator phase transition in low-dimensional systems, with successful quantitative comparison to experiments. Published in Physical Review B (2025).
Monte Carlo Methods & Critical Phenomena
Extensive experience with Metropolis, Demon, Wolff, Quantum, Rotor Monte Carlo, and Wang–Landau algorithms. Studied Wang–Landau efficiency for self-gravitating gases. Developed a rotor Monte Carlo method to extract critical exponents at a topological phase transition in a breathing kagome lattice.
Quantum Information & Entanglement
Quantified entanglement-spectrum statistics in coupled kicked-top Floquet dynamics using Random Matrix Theory, demonstrating fixed-trace Wishart–Laguerre agreement across the regular–chaotic transition.
High Energy Physics & Scattering Theory
Proton geometry modeling using quantum chromodynamics (QCD) to explain vector meson production at the hard-scattering limit. Published in Physical Review C (2019).
